Exercise 57: Creating 10 simple displays for your light sculpture

 

DESCRIPTION:

 

In the previous exercises we have learned all the basics that are required to program your light sculpture. Now it is time to begin making 10 simple displays that will be the starting point for your light sculpture program. Once you have those 10 simple displays, we can insert them into a template for the light sculpture program and then you will be well along in creating your own unique program for your light sculpture.

 

Ideas for your 10 simple displays could include but are not limited to:

  • Lighting up each Color
  • Slowly lighting up each LED one at a time
  • Going around / spinning
  • Stair Step
  • Stair Fall
  • Lights chasing one another
  • Lights colliding and falling back
  • Lights colliding and splitting
  • Lights colliding then splitting then re-combining

 

Requirements for your 10 simple displays

  • The combined runtime of all 10 simple displays must be at least a minute, several minutes is better.
  • The first display must be some sort of introduction (i.e. starting slow, showing off your colors or shapes).
  • The displays must build on each other (i.e. start slow, then speeding up, or perhaps start small then go bigger).  Try to make each display a little more interesting than the last.
  • The displays must be different from each other.  Going forward then backwards can be a good thing to do, but that might count only as one display.
  • Each simple display MUST be a Function that uses at least three arguments(typically onTime, offTime, repeatCount).
  • Each simple display MUST use loops and arrays wherever possible.
  • Create all your 10 Simple Displays in One Arduino Sketch - YourInitials_10Simple in: Documents – Arduino – Exercises

 

ACTIVITIES:

  3. This would be a good time to use the 3D light Sculpture Simulator to test your code. You can input your rod locations, lengths, and colors, then use the edit code button to paste in your Simple functions to test them out.
